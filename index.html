A creative ambivert based in Manchester

Hi there! I'm Emily - a 26 year old junior web developer - a self professed skill sponge, and the type of person with a creative mindset yet loves organisation and structure. Growing up, I was always an avid artist and reader and that passion has not faded as I've grown.

Since my redundancy during the pandemic, and overcoming my 10 year long struggle with depression, I am finally setting my sights on finding a career that suits me. As someone that values functionality, efficiency and aesthetics, I have yet to find a role that combines my creative flair and ingenuity. This is where UI/UX Design comes in. I am keen to further explore, practise and specialise in an area that utilises my passions. I still have a lot to learn, but that simply excites me more.

My educational background illustrates my love for understanding how people work as well as utilising my artistic desideratum. Having studied Psychology, Art and English Literature at A Level and then going on to studying English Literature & East Asian Studies at the University of Leeds - I have a keen fascination for the way people think, feel and behave. The emergence of technology and how people interact with the technology is a behaviour that intrigues me daily.

The growth of technology, how it looks, how it works, how people interact with it, is changing constantly. To interactivity, the emergence of both VR and AR, and all the results from these interactions enriching the lives of everyday people is astounding.

I am merely on the start of my own journey, and in this I hope to create things that would fulfill the lives of others.

Although my previous role as a Sales Operations Specialist satisfied my itch for efficiency and organisation. My creativity took a backseat and left me unfulfilled. In my spare time I would dip my toes into Adobe InDesign, and honing my digital art skills via practise and online guides. I hope to continue my pursuits in conjunction with my career and branch out into other design tools such as Sketch, SketchUp, wireframing and InVision.

I have so much more to learn and am eager to grow as the industry continues to blossom. My commitment to always producing the best work that I can possibly give, and to never stop learning, especially in an area that has sparked my interest defines me as a person.
